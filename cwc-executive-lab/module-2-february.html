Framework

The Win-Tracking System

Every win should answer three questions. This framework makes your impact undeniable.

WHAT

What did you accomplish? Be specific about the deliverable, project, or action you completed.

SO WHAT

What was the business impact? Quantify with metrics, revenue, time saved, or problems solved.

NOW WHAT

What's next? Connect this win to future goals, larger initiatives, or promotion criteria.

Corporate Example

WHAT: "Led the Q1 product launch"
SO WHAT: "Generated $2M in new revenue and 15% customer growth"
NOW WHAT: "Positioning us to expand into enterprise sales—a key strategic priority"

Non-Profit Example

WHAT: "Redesigned our volunteer onboarding program"
SO WHAT: "Increased volunteer retention by 40% and reduced training time by 2 weeks"
NOW WHAT: "Enabling us to scale our community outreach initiative ahead of schedule"

  • What does "promotable" look like here?
  • Who decides my next opportunity?
  • How do I authentically reach them?

Relationship Capital

Your Sponsor Pipeline

Identify 3 internal stakeholders who should consistently see your wins. These are your future sponsors.

Your Manager

The first line of advocacy. Ensure they have the evidence to fight for you in calibration meetings.

Skip-Level Leader

Your manager's manager. They have more influence in promotion decisions than you think.

Cross-Functional Leader

Someone outside your team who can speak to your impact across the organization.

This month's goal: Ensure each of these three people knows at least one recent win of yours.

Summer Handouts Guide

July & August | Executive Leadership Lab

Summer Integration Period

July and August serve as a pause from live modules, giving participants time to integrate the learning from the first half of the year (January-June) while continuing to build relationships and community.

During this period, provide these six handouts for self-paced work, along with community engagement activities to maintain momentum.

1

Board of Advisors Deep Dive

Purpose: Expand on June's Board of Advisors introduction with comprehensive relationship mapping and actionable outreach strategies.

What to Include

  • Advisor Categories: Mentor, Sponsor, Peer Ally, Coach, Industry Connector
  • Relationship Mapping Worksheet: Current advisors vs. gaps to fill
  • Outreach Templates: Email/LinkedIn scripts for cold and warm outreach
  • 60-Day Nurture Calendar: Weekly touchpoint schedule for relationship building
  • Conversation Starters: What to talk about beyond "Can you help me?"
  • Giving First Strategies: How to add value before asking for anything
WOC Focus: Include guidance on building cross-cultural relationships, navigating spaces where advisors don't "look like you," and finding advisors who understand the WOC experience.
2

Sponsor vs. Mentor Guide

Purpose: Clarify the critical difference between sponsors and mentors, and provide strategies for finding and cultivating sponsors.

What to Include

  • The Difference: Mentors advise; Sponsors advocate. Mentors talk TO you; Sponsors talk ABOUT you.
  • Why Sponsors Matter More: Research on sponsorship and promotion rates
  • The Sponsorship Gap: Why WOC are over-mentored and under-sponsored
  • Finding Potential Sponsors: Who has power, influence, and access?
  • Earning Sponsorship: You can't ask for a sponsor—you earn one
  • Converting Mentors to Sponsors: Strategies for deepening relationships
  • Making It Easy to Sponsor You: Giving sponsors the language and evidence they need
WOC Focus: Address the reality that sponsors are often white men with power, and how to navigate those relationships authentically without code-switching away your identity.
3

Mid-Year Reflection Workbook

Purpose: Structured reflection on January-June progress to consolidate learning and prepare for the second half.

What to Include

  • Win Inventory: Compile all wins tracked since January
  • Goal Check-In: Review Career Growth Roadmap goals—what's on track, what needs adjustment?
  • Visibility Audit: Who now knows your work that didn't in January?
  • Skills Assessment: What new capabilities have you developed?
  • Relationship Inventory: Who's in your network now vs. January?
  • Lessons Learned: What worked? What didn't? What surprised you?
  • Second-Half Intentions: What do you want from September-December?
  • One Big Question: What's the one thing you still need to figure out?
WOC Focus: Include reflection on how identity has shown up in their journey—moments of pride, moments of challenge, and how they've navigated both.
4

Networking for Introverts

Purpose: Authentic networking strategies for those who find traditional networking draining or inauthentic.

What to Include

  • Redefining Networking: It's relationship-building, not collecting business cards
  • Quality Over Quantity: Deep connections beat wide networks
  • Energy Management: How to network without depleting yourself
  • One-on-One Strategies: Coffee chats, virtual meetings, walking meetings
  • Written Networking: Thoughtful emails, LinkedIn engagement, commenting on content
  • Event Survival Guide: How to work a room when you'd rather leave
  • Follow-Up Systems: Maintaining relationships without constant socializing
  • Permission to Be Yourself: Networking doesn't require becoming an extrovert
WOC Focus: Address the additional energy cost of networking in predominantly white spaces, and strategies for finding community and belonging.
5

The Reciprocity Playbook

Purpose: Teach participants how to add value to their network, building genuine relationships rather than transactional ones.

What to Include

  • The Giving Mindset: Lead with value, not asks
  • 25 Ways to Add Value: Specific actions that help others (introductions, sharing resources, amplifying work, etc.)
  • The 5-Minute Favor: Small acts that make a big impact
  • Connecting Others: Being a bridge in your network
  • Sharing Your Expertise: How to offer help without overcommitting
  • Celebrating Others: Public recognition, recommendations, endorsements
  • The Reciprocity Bank: Tracking how you've added value (not to keep score, but to remind yourself of your generosity)
  • When to Ask: How giving creates space for receiving
WOC Focus: Balance giving generously with protecting your energy—address the tendency to over-give and under-receive.
6

Summer Reading List

Purpose: Curated resources for continued learning during the summer pause, with discussion prompts for community engagement.

Suggested Categories

  • Leadership & Executive Presence (2-3 books)
  • Career Strategy & Advancement (2-3 books)
  • The WOC Experience (2-3 books by/for women of color)
  • Negotiation & Influence (1-2 books)
  • Wellness & Sustainability (1-2 books)
  • Podcasts & Articles (curated list for those who prefer audio/shorter content)

For Each Resource, Include

  • Brief description (2-3 sentences)
  • Why it's relevant to the program
  • 2-3 discussion questions for the community
  • Key takeaway to look for
WOC Focus: Center books by women of color authors and ensure the reading list reflects diverse perspectives, not just mainstream leadership advice.

Community Engagement Activities

Keep the community active during the summer pause with these low-lift engagement strategies:

Accountability Partner Pairings

Match participants in pairs for bi-weekly summer check-ins. Provide a simple agenda: share one win, one challenge, one goal for next two weeks.

Weekly Skool Discussion Prompts

Post one thought-provoking question each week to keep conversation flowing. Rotate between reflection, celebration, and advice-seeking.

Win of the Week Thread

Casual weekly thread where participants share wins—big or small. Creates habit of celebrating and builds community support.

Optional Book Club Meetup

One virtual gathering mid-summer to discuss summer reading. Keep it informal—45 minutes max.

Suggested Summer Schedule

Week 1 Release Handouts 1-3 + Pair accountability partners
Week 2 Discussion prompt: "Biggest lesson from the first half?"
Week 3 Release Handouts 4-6 + Win of the Week thread
Week 4 Discussion prompt: "Who's one person you want to connect with?"
Week 5 Accountability partner check-in reminder
Week 6 Optional Book Club meetup
Week 7 Discussion prompt: "What are you most excited about for September?"
Week 8 Mid-Year Reflection sharing + Preview of September module

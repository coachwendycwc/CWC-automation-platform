Slide 1: Title Slide

1-2 minutes

Opening Script

"Welcome to Module 2 of the Executive Leadership Lab. This month we're tackling something that trips up so many talented women of color—the gap between doing excellent work and actually being recognized for it.

Today's session is called Strategic Positioning for Promotion, and by the end, you'll have a concrete visibility plan and the tools to make sure your work doesn't go unnoticed."

Engagement: "Before we dive in, drop in the chat: On a scale of 1-10, how visible do you think your work is to decision-makers right now?"

Slide 2: Welcome - Your Work Deserves Recognition

2-3 minutes
Key Points:
  • Acknowledge their hard work and results
  • The stats are stark: 58% of WOC feel overlooked, 2x less likely to be promoted
  • Today we're going to change that narrative

Script

"Look at these numbers. 58% of women of color report feeling overlooked for advancement. We're twice as likely to be passed over for promotion even when our performance is equal or better.

This isn't about working harder—you're already doing that. This is about working smarter with your visibility. Today, we fix that gap."

Engagement: "Raise your hand or put a '1' in chat if you've ever felt like your work went unnoticed while someone else got the credit."

Slide 3: Objectives - What You'll Gain

2 minutes
Walk through the four objectives:
  • Create a Visibility Plan - Strategic, tied to business outcomes
  • Build a Win-Tracking System - Your "receipts" for promotion conversations
  • Master Performance Conversations - Own check-ins and reviews
  • Identify Your Sponsor Pipeline - Know who needs to see your work

Script

"By the end of today, you'll walk away with four concrete deliverables. These aren't theoretical—you'll actually build these during our session together."

Slide 4: The Challenge - Excellence Without Visibility

3 minutes
Three archetypes that keep us stuck:
  • The Silent Achiever - Great work, no one knows
  • The Untracked Wins - Can't remember at review time
  • The Missing Advocates - No one fighting for you in the room

Script

"Which of these sounds familiar? The Silent Achiever who delivers exceptional results but no one outside the immediate team knows their name? The one who can't remember their wins at review time? Or the one who realizes no one was advocating for them when the promotion decision was made?

Good work doesn't speak for itself. Good work needs a microphone. And today, we're going to give you that microphone."

Engagement: "In the chat, which of these three resonates most with where you are right now?"

Slide 5: Framework - The Visibility Equation

3-4 minutes
The Core Framework:

Performance + Positioning + Promotion Criteria = Advancement

Most high performers only focus on Performance. We need all three.

Script

"Here's the framework that changes everything. Advancement isn't just about what you do—it's about who knows what you do AND whether it aligns with what actually gets rewarded.

Most of us have been focused entirely on performance. We thought if we just worked hard enough, someone would notice. But that's only one-third of the equation.

Today we address all three parts."

Pause Point: "Take a moment—which of these three areas do you think is your biggest gap right now? Performance documentation? Positioning? Or understanding your promotion criteria?"

Slide 6: Deep Dive - Know Your Promotion Criteria

4 minutes

Script

"Let's start with promotion criteria. You cannot hit a target you cannot see.

How many of you actually know what your organization's documented promotion criteria are? Not what you assume they are—what's actually written down?

If you don't know what 'exceeds expectations' means at your company, if you're guessing what leadership values, if your goals don't align with documented competencies—you're playing a game without knowing the rules.

This week, I want you to find out. Ask your manager directly: 'What does promotable look like in the next cycle?' Get specific."

Slide 7: Deep Dive - Strategic Visibility

4 minutes

Script

"Strategic visibility isn't about being loud or self-promotional in a way that feels inauthentic. It's about being intentional.

Who makes promotion decisions at your organization? Is it just your manager, or is there a calibration committee? Who's in that room when your name comes up?

If decision-makers don't know your name, if you're not invited to strategic conversations, if opportunities keep going to people who are less qualified but more visible—that's a visibility problem, not a performance problem."

Slide 8: Deep Dive - Win Documentation

3 minutes

Script

"Here's a truth bomb: Your manager has too many direct reports and too much on their plate to remember every good thing you've done. That's not their fault—it's just reality.

So when they go into that calibration meeting to advocate for your promotion, what evidence do they have? Only what you've given them.

If you're scrambling to write self-assessments, if you're forgetting accomplishments from Q1 by Q4—you're leaving your advancement to chance. We're going to fix that today."

Slide 9: Quote Slide

1 minute

Script

"I want you to sit with this for a moment: 'Visibility isn't bragging. It's ensuring your contributions are known by those who make decisions about your future.'

This is a reframe many of us need. We've been taught that self-promotion is arrogant. But staying silent about your contributions isn't humble—it's a disservice to your career and to the organization that needs to know who their high performers are."

Slide 10: Root Causes - Why Your Work Goes Unseen

3-4 minutes
Six root causes:
  • Modesty culture - We were taught that talking about ourselves is inappropriate
  • No tracking system - Without a system, wins just disappear
  • Wrong audience - Your manager knows, but the people who make decisions don't
  • Inconsistent communication - You spike at review time but go silent the rest of the year
  • Fear of self-promotion - Worrying about being seen as 'too much'
  • Waiting to be noticed - Believing hard work will automatically be recognized
Engagement: "Which of these is your biggest barrier? Be honest with yourself."

Slide 11: Activity - Visibility Diagnostic

7-8 minutes

Facilitation

"Now we're going to do a quick diagnostic. I'm giving you 5 minutes.

Part 1: List everyone who currently knows about your work and impact. How far does your visibility actually reach?

Part 2: Now identify the decision-makers and stakeholders who SHOULD know your work but don't. These are your visibility gaps.

I'll put you in breakout rooms of 3-4 people. Share what you discovered. You have 5 minutes—go!"

Debrief: "What did you discover? Anyone willing to share a gap they identified?"

Slide 12: Framework - The Win-Tracking System

4-5 minutes
WHAT / SO WHAT / NOW WHAT Framework:
  • WHAT - What did you accomplish? Be specific.
  • SO WHAT - What was the business impact? Metrics, revenue, time saved.
  • NOW WHAT - How does this connect to future goals or promotion criteria?

Examples to Share

Corporate Example Non-Profit Example
WHAT: "I led the Q1 product launch"
SO WHAT: "Generated $2M in new revenue and 15% customer growth"
NOW WHAT: "Positioning us to expand into enterprise sales" 		WHAT: "Redesigned volunteer onboarding"
SO WHAT: "Increased retention by 40%, reduced training by 2 weeks"
NOW WHAT: "Enabling us to scale community outreach ahead of schedule"

Slide 13: Activity - Start Your Win Log

12-15 minutes

Facilitation

"Now it's your turn. Using the What / So What / Now What framework, I want you to capture 3-5 wins from the past 30 days.

I'm sharing the Win Tracking Template in the chat now. You have 10 minutes. Don't overthink it—just get the wins documented.

Pro tip: Set a weekly 5-minute calendar reminder to update this. Friday afternoons work well—you're wrapping up the week anyway."

Share in Chat:
  • Win Tracking Template link

Slides 14-15: Visibility Strategy Checklist & Activity

23-28 minutes total
Six-Step System:
  • Step 1: Promotion Criteria - Document what your org rewards
  • Step 2: Key Stakeholders - Identify 3-5 decision-makers
  • Step 3: Communication Channels - How will you reach them?
  • Step 4: Frequency - How often will you share?
  • Step 5: Win Categories - What types of wins to highlight
  • Step 6: Review Rhythm - Monthly visibility check

Activity Facilitation

"This is the deep work. I'm sharing the Visibility Strategy Checklist in the chat. Work through each section. Be specific—this becomes your playbook for the next year.

You have 20 minutes. I'll play some focus music. If you finish early, pair up in the chat to share your plans."

Slide 16: Performance Management Cycle

4 minutes

Script

"Most people treat performance reviews like a test you cram for the night before. But the winners treat it like a year-long campaign.

At goal-setting, you should already be aligning your work to promotion criteria. Throughout the year, you're tracking wins. At mid-cycle, you're course-correcting. Your self-assessment reads like a promotion case. And before calibration, you're arming your manager with specific examples they can use to advocate for you."

PhaseVisibility Action
Goal SettingAlign your goals to promotion criteria from day one
Throughout CycleTrack wins in real-time—don't scramble at review time
Mid-Cycle ReviewRequest feedback check-in to course-correct early
Self-AssessmentWrite it like a promotion case with metrics and impact
Calibration PrepGive your manager specific examples they can advocate with

Slide 17: Maximize Your Check-Ins

4-5 minutes
The "Win + Ask" Formula:

WIN: "I completed X, which resulted in Y impact"
ASK: "I'd like to take on Z to build toward [goal]"

Power Questions to Memorize:
  • "What would make me promotable in the next cycle?"
  • "Who else should know about this work?"
  • "Are there stretch opportunities I should pursue?"

Slide 19: Your Sponsor Pipeline

4 minutes
Three people who should consistently see your wins:
  • Your Manager - First line of advocacy. Ensure they have evidence to fight for you.
  • Skip-Level Leader - Your manager's manager. More influence than you think.
  • Cross-Functional Leader - Someone outside your team who can speak to your impact.
This month's goal: Each of these three people should know at least one recent win of yours.

Slide 20: 14-Day Visibility Sprint

3 minutes
Three actions for the next two weeks:
  • This week: Share one win—email your manager or mention it in a team meeting
  • Next week: Schedule a check-in using the prep template
  • By day 14: Add 5 wins to your tracker (backfill from past 60 days if needed)

Slides 21-22: Quote & Summary

3-4 minutes

Quote Script

"'You can't be considered for opportunities people don't know you want.'

Read that again. If you haven't explicitly told the right people what you want—not hinted, not hoped they'd figure it out—you're not in the running. Your ambition is not obvious. You have to make it known."

Five Key Takeaways:
  • Visibility is strategic, not accidental - It requires a plan
  • Track wins in real-time - If you don't document it, it didn't happen
  • Own your performance conversations - Check-ins are visibility opportunities
  • Know who needs to see your work - Build your sponsor pipeline now
  • Consistency beats one-time efforts - Small actions compound over time

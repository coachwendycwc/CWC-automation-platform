Module 5 — May

Negotiation Scripts by Scenario

Four complete scripts for four common negotiation situations. Adapt to your voice — the structure is non-negotiable.

How to use these scripts

Yellow brackets are your fill-in fields. Read each full script aloud at least twice before your conversation. The goal is not memorization — it is muscle memory. When you know the structure, the words come naturally.

Scenario 1

Annual Review — Salary Increase Request

You're in your current role and it's review time (or you're requesting a review meeting).
Open — Set the context. No apology.
"I appreciate you making time for this, [Name]. I'd like to have a direct conversation about my compensation and make sure it reflects my contributions and aligns with the market."
Case — Present your evidence
"I've done some market research. Based on my role, my [X years] of experience, and the scope of my work in [location/industry], the market range is [$X to $Y]. My current compensation of [$Z] is below that range.

Beyond the market data — over the past year I [Win #1 with impact], and I also [Win #2 with impact]. Those contributions represent significant value to this organization."
Ask — Say the number. Stop talking. Wait.
"Based on that, I'm asking for a base salary of [$specific number]. That would bring me to market and reflect the level I'm delivering at."
Close
"I'd love to hear your thoughts. I'm committed to continuing to deliver at this level — I want to make sure my compensation reflects that commitment."
After they respond: If they need to check with someone — nail down a timeline. "I appreciate that. Can we plan to reconnect by [specific date] with an answer?"
Scenario 2

New Job Offer — Counteroffer Script

You've received an offer and want to negotiate before accepting.
Initial response (call or email — buy time)
"Thank you so much — I'm genuinely excited about this opportunity and [something specific you're excited about]. I've received the offer and I'd like to take a day to review it fully. Is it okay if I follow up with you by [specific date]?"
Counteroffer script
"I've reviewed the offer carefully, and I want to reiterate how excited I am about this role. I'd like to share some context and make a counteroffer.

Based on my research into market compensation for [title] in [location/industry], as well as my [X years] of experience and [specific expertise], I was expecting a base in the range of [$X to $Y]. The current offer of [$Z] is below that.

I'm asking for a base of [$specific number]. I'm confident I'll bring that value from day one."
If they say the base is firm — expand the table:
"I appreciate you being direct about the base. Can we talk about other elements? I'm also interested in discussing [signing bonus / equity / PTO / remote flexibility]. Is there flexibility there?"
WOC Note

Research shows women of color receive lower initial offers than white counterparts for the same role. Your instinct may be to accept quickly out of gratitude. Resist. A well-delivered counteroffer signals confidence, not ingratitude.

Scenario 3

Requesting a Promotion

You want a title change — with or without an immediate compensation increase.
Open
"I want to have a direct conversation about my career path and, specifically, about a promotion to [target title]. I've been thinking about the right time to bring this up, and I believe I've built a strong case."
Case — Show you're already operating at that level
"Here's what I've been doing at that level already: [2–3 specific examples of work beyond your current title]. I've also taken on [scope or leadership examples], which is typically associated with [target title] responsibilities.

From a market standpoint, someone in a [target title] role with my scope typically earns between [$X and $Y]. My current compensation doesn't reflect the level I'm operating at."
Ask
"I'm asking to be formally promoted to [target title], with a corresponding compensation adjustment to [$specific number]."
If they say "not yet":
"I appreciate you being honest. Can you help me understand exactly what 'ready' looks like? I'd like specific milestones so I can demonstrate that clearly — and can we agree on a 90-day check-in to revisit this?"
Scenario 4

Negotiating Beyond Salary

Scope, flexibility, development budget, equity — the full table is bigger than base pay.
Scope expansion ask
"I want to talk about the scope of my role. I've been leading [project/function], which is [description of expanded scope]. I'd like to formally expand my responsibilities to include [specific area], and I'd like that reflected in my title and compensation."
Remote / schedule flexibility ask
"I'd like to discuss a [fully remote / hybrid / compressed schedule] arrangement. I've been delivering strong results — [specific example] — and I believe this flexibility would actually increase my productivity and sustainability in this role. Here's what I'm proposing: [specific arrangement]."
Professional development budget ask
"I'd like to invest in [certification / coaching / conference] this year, and I'm requesting [$specific amount] in professional development support. Here's how it connects: [specific connection to role impact]. I see this as a return on investment for the team, not just for me."
Key principle: Non-cash asks should always be tied to organizational value — not just personal preference. Frame every ask as "here is how this benefits the team or mission." It reframes the conversation from a favor to an investment.

Module 5 — May

Compensation Research Guide

Know what the market says before you walk in the room. Data is confidence.

Before you research

You cannot negotiate effectively with feelings alone. Pull data from at least 3 sources, log it in the worksheet at the bottom, and synthesize your market range before drafting your script.

1
Where to Research
Six sources — use at least three. Check off each one you've consulted.
1

Glassdoor

glassdoor.com/Salaries
Free
  • Search by exact job title AND location for the most accurate data
  • Filter by company size to match your context
  • Look at Base Pay + Additional Pay (bonus, equity) for total picture
2

LinkedIn Salary

linkedin.com/salary
Free with Premium
  • Use experience filters — years of experience changes the estimate significantly
  • Check median AND 75th percentile — you want to know what top performers earn
  • Cross-reference with active job postings in your area
3

Levels.fyi

levels.fyi
FreeTech / Corporate
  • Best for roles with equity — shows total comp (base + bonus + RSUs)
  • Compare TC (total comp), not just base salary
  • Pay attention to level equivalencies across companies
4

Bureau of Labor Statistics

bls.gov/oes
FreeGovernment Data
  • Use the Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS) tool
  • Filter by state and metro area for geographic accuracy
  • Supplement with real-time sources — BLS data updates slowly
5

Payscale

payscale.com
Free / Paid
  • The "What Am I Worth" tool builds personalized reports by skill set
  • Input your full skill set — each additional skill can shift the estimate
6

Your Network

Trusted colleagues, recruiters, professional associations
Free
  • Ask: "Are you comfortable sharing your comp range for [role]?"
  • Recruiters see hundreds of offers — they are often the most accurate source
  • Other WOC in comparable roles are your best benchmark
WOC Research Note

Salary databases often reflect historical wage gaps — which means the "median" may underrepresent your actual market value. Supplement public data with network conversations, especially with women of color in comparable roles. They are your most accurate benchmark.

How to Synthesize Your Research
Don't just average the numbers. Follow this process.
1

Pull data from at least 3 sources

Log each source's range in the worksheet below. The overlap between sources becomes your credible range.

2

Find where 3+ sources agree — that's your anchor

Look for convergence, not the average. The zone where multiple sources cluster is the defensible range.

3

Position yourself based on your experience and impact

If you're delivering top-quartile results, your ask belongs in the top quartile of the range — not the median.

4

Set your target at the ceiling of your acceptable range

You will almost certainly be countered. Specific asks (e.g., $127,500 not "around $125K") outperform ranges every time.

5

Decide your walk-away number — before the meeting

Know the minimum you'll accept. Keep it private. Anything below that is a signal about the ceiling in this space.

Negotiation Meeting Plan

Plan the meeting before you request it. Preparation is the difference between a conversation and a negotiation.

Part 1: Meeting Setup
Fill this in before you request the meeting.
Meeting Request Message Template
"Hi [Name], I'd love to schedule 30 minutes to talk through my role and contributions. I have some things I'd like to discuss and value your perspective. Are you available [date/time options]?"
Tip: Keep the subject line neutral. "Compensation Discussion" or "Salary Review" can trigger defensiveness before you're even in the room. "Career Check-In" or "Professional Development Conversation" keeps the door open.
Part 2: Meeting Agenda
Know the arc of your 30 minutes before you walk in.
1

Minutes 0–3: Open and set the tone

Express appreciation for the time. Anchor to the relationship. State the purpose directly and professionally.

2

Minutes 3–10: Present the case

Market research → Your impact evidence → Your ask. Deliver the full four-part script without rushing.

3

Minutes 10–12: State the ask and stop talking

Say the number. Close the script. Then wait. Do not fill the silence.

4

Minutes 12–25: Respond and navigate

Listen to their response. Use your objection response cards if needed. Stay calm, direct, and grounded.

5

Minutes 25–30: Close with a clear next step

Regardless of the outcome, leave with something specific: a follow-up date, a milestone list, or written confirmation.

Part 3: Mindset Prep
Complete before the meeting. What you think going in shapes what happens in the room.
  • Negotiation Preparation Worksheet complete
  • Script practiced aloud at least 3 times
  • Market research data ready to reference (don't read from it — know it)
  • Top 2–3 impact wins memorized
  • Objection responses reviewed
  • Know my walk-away minimum
  • Know my specific ask — not a range, a number
Part 4: Post-Meeting Notes
Complete within 1 hour of the meeting — while details are fresh.

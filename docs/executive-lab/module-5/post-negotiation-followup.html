Post-Negotiation Follow-Up Template

What you do after the ask matters as much as the ask itself. Every outcome has a next step.

What was the outcome of your negotiation?

✅ Yes They agreed (fully or partially)
⏳ Not Yet Delayed, conditional, or pending
🚧 No Declined — no path offered
Outcome: Yes — Get It in Writing
Congratulations. Now protect it. The follow-up email is not optional.
Follow-Up Email Template — Send Within 24 Hours
Subject: Re: Our Conversation — Thank You

Hi [Name],

Thank you for meeting with me and for the conversation today. I wanted to confirm what we discussed: [summary of what was agreed — be specific: title, salary, scope, effective date].

I'm excited to continue delivering at this level, and I appreciate your confidence in my contributions. I look forward to receiving the updated [offer letter / compensation confirmation] by [specific date].

Thank you again.

[Your Name]
Important: Do not consider the negotiation complete until it is confirmed in writing. Verbal agreements change. Managers leave. HR loses notes. Your email creates the paper trail that protects you.
Outcome: Not Yet — Make Vague Into Specific
A "not yet" without milestones is a slow no. Your job now is to convert vague into accountable.
Follow-Up Email Template — Send Within 24 Hours
Subject: Follow-Up — Our Conversation on [Date]

Hi [Name],

Thank you for your time and for being direct with me. I appreciate your transparency.

I wanted to follow up and confirm: based on our conversation, we agreed to revisit my [compensation / title / scope] in [timeline — be specific: "Q2 review" / "90 days" / "by April 15"].

To make sure I'm on the right path, I'd like to confirm the specific milestones or criteria: [list what they said OR write "I want to make sure I understand what 'ready' looks like — can you share 2–3 specific indicators?"].

Can we schedule a brief check-in for [specific date] to review progress?

[Your Name]

Outcome: No — This Is Information
A flat no — with no explanation and no path — tells you something about this organization. Use it wisely.
Three questions to ask yourself about a flat no:
  • Was this decision-maker the actual decision-maker — or do I need to escalate?
  • Is the ceiling structural (the org genuinely can't support this) or is it person-specific?
  • What does this tell me about my long-term trajectory here?
Follow-Up Email Template — Send Within 24 Hours
Subject: Follow-Up — Our Conversation

Hi [Name],

Thank you for your candor during our conversation. I appreciate you taking the time.

While I'm disappointed by the outcome, I want to make sure I'm using this as a learning opportunity. To help me understand the decision fully: [Is there a specific path that would change the answer? / Can you help me understand what would need to be true for this to be reconsidered?].

I remain committed to my work here, and I'd value any guidance you can offer on how to position myself going forward.

[Your Name]

The Core Idea

A Board, Not a Person

No single mentor can be your strategist, your advocate, your connector, and your truth-teller. So stop looking for one person. Build a board.

The idea of a personal board of directors — instead of one all-purpose mentor — was popularized by Priscilla Claman in Harvard Business Review (2010). The roles we'll define next are this program's framework.

The Framework

Mentor · Sponsor · Advocate

Mentor

Talks to you

Gives advice, perspective, and guidance. Helps you think. Does not move the decision.

Sponsor

Talks about you

Advocates for you when you're not in the room and creates opportunities for you.

Advocate / Connector

Opens the network

Vouches for you laterally and connects you to people and rooms you can't reach alone.

A sponsor, defined: “someone other than your direct manager who actively advocates for your career advancement and/or creates opportunities for you.”

Definition: McKinsey & LeanIn, 2025

Why Sponsorship

Sponsorship Has a Measurable Effect

Mentorship is advice. Sponsorship is action — and it shows up in promotions.

+23%
Men with a sponsor — more likely to get the next promotion
+19%
Women with a sponsor — more likely to get the next promotion

Sylvia Ann Hewlett, The Sponsor Effect, 2010

Note the gap in the benefit itself: women get a real but smaller bump. That's exactly why building your board deliberately matters.

The Conversion

From Mentor to Sponsor

You don't earn a sponsor by asking “will you sponsor me.” You earn one by making advocacy easy and specific.

1

Deliver visible wins

Give them something concrete to point to.

2

Make the ask specific

Name the role, the room, or the opportunity.

3

Make it easy to say yes

Give them the words and the why.

Module 6 — June
Strategy

Building Your Board as a Woman of Color

The Realities

  • The “prove-it-again” tax means your wins need to be documented and visible.
  • Visibility is not the same as advocacy — being seen isn't being championed.
  • Access to senior leaders is uneven, as the data this module already showed.

The Moves

  • Bring receipts — pair every ask with a documented win.
  • Build both cross-racial sponsors and in-community advocates.
  • Convert visibility into advocacy with a specific ask.

Sustain It

A Board Is a Relationship, Not a Transaction

Give value back

Offer help, intros, and recognition — not just asks.

Stay in contact

Regular, light touches beat a flurry only when you need something.

Close the loop

Update them on the wins they helped create.

The most common mistake: going silent until you need a favor. Reciprocity keeps the board alive.

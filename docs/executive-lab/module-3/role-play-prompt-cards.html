Role-Play Prompt Cards

Practice handling the toughest pivot conversations before they happen for real.

How to Use These Cards

Each card presents a scenario you'll likely face during your career pivot. For each one:

  • Read the scenario and the other person's line
  • Review the strategy tips
  • Write your response in your own words
  • Practice saying it out loud (with a partner if possible)
  • Rate your confidence and refine

The Skeptic

When someone questions your decision to pivot

They Say:

"Why would you leave [your current field]? You've built so much there. That seems like a big risk."

Your Strategy

  • Lead with your vision, not your frustration
  • Reframe the pivot as strategic growth, not escape
  • Show that you've done your research
  • Don't be defensive—confidence is your best answer

The Gatekeeper

When someone says you don't have the "right" experience

They Say:

"We usually look for candidates with direct experience in [target field]. How would you bridge that gap?"

Your Strategy

  • Acknowledge the question without conceding the point
  • Bridge with specific, transferable accomplishments
  • Use the "same skill, different context" reframe
  • Position your outside perspective as a competitive advantage

The Networking Event

When you need to introduce yourself in pivot mode

They Say:

"So, what do you do?"

Your Strategy

  • Lead with where you're going, not just where you've been
  • Use a forward-looking introduction
  • Include a hook that invites follow-up questions
  • Keep it to 15–20 seconds—less is more

The Interview

When you need to deliver your full pivot story

They Say:

"Walk me through your career trajectory and why you're making this change."

Your Strategy

  • Use the full four-part Pivot Story Script (Hook, Bridge, Evidence, Vision)
  • Keep it to 60–90 seconds
  • Maintain a narrative arc—don't just list jobs
  • End strong with your vision for the future, not a recap of the past

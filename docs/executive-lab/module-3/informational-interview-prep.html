Informational Interview Prep Guide

Research, plan, and execute informational interviews that accelerate your career pivot.

How to Use This Worksheet

Complete the research section first, then use the contact tracker for each person you plan to reach out to. Prep each conversation using the question bank. Your answers auto-save as you type.

1

Research Your Target Space

Before reaching out, know what you're walking into. Do your homework so your conversations are strategic, not aimless.

2

3

Question Bank

Choose 5–7 questions per conversation. Start with rapport-building questions, move to insight questions, and close with connection questions.

Rapport & Opening

  • How did you get into this field? What was your path?
  • What do you enjoy most about your current role?

Insider Insight

  • What skills matter most in this role that aren't in the job description?
  • What would someone from my background need to demonstrate to be taken seriously?
  • What's the biggest misconception about transitioning into this field?
  • What does success look like in the first 90 days of this type of role?
  • What are the biggest challenges facing this industry right now?

Connection & Closing

  • Who else should I be talking to? (warm introductions)
  • Are there communities, events, or publications I should follow?
  • Would it be okay if I followed up with you as I make progress?
4

Your Outreach Message

Draft a reusable outreach template. Keep it short, specific, and easy to say yes to.

Follow-Up Protocol

1. Send a thank-you within 24 hours. 2. Reference something specific they said. 3. Share a relevant article or resource within a week. 4. Update them on your progress in 30 days. This turns one conversation into a lasting relationship.

5

Conversation Debrief

After each informational interview, capture your key takeaways while they're fresh.
